Dreamy Sleep started as a **personal project**.

Like many people, I wanted something simple to play in the background at night:
soft sounds, steady volume, no surprises. I often used YouTube, but over time
the friction became obvious—ads, interruptions, bright screens, and no reliable
way to stop playback once sleep had settled in.

So I built something small for myself.

What began as a personal sleep aid later turned out to be something that could
also help **new parents**, especially during those quiet, repetitive nights with
a sleeping newborn.

---

## A Simple Idea: Play Calm Sound, Then Disappear

Dreamy Sleep is a lightweight, browser‑based sound app designed to do one thing
well:

**play calm background audio, quietly and continuously, and then get out of the
way.**

There’s no account system, no recommendations, and no content feed. You open the
page, choose a sound, press play, and put the device aside.

## Continuous Background Sounds

<img
  src='/images/posts/dreamy-sleep/dreamy-sleep-player.png'
  alt='Dreamy Sleep Sound Player'
  className='rounded-xl'
/>

All sounds in Dreamy Sleep loop seamlessly using native browser audio. There are
no gaps, no silence between loops, and no abrupt changes—important whether
you’re trying to relax yourself or keep a sleeping baby undisturbed.

Available sound options include:

### Universal Sounds

- 🎵 Lullaby
- 🌧️ Rain
- ☕ Coffee shop ambience
- 🌊 White noise

These are neutral, stable sounds designed to fade into the background rather
than command attention.

---

## Gentle Visual Atmosphere

The background includes slow, ambient animations—stars, rain, bubbles, musical
notes, and other soft elements depending on the selected sound.

These visuals are optional by design:

- They move slowly
- They avoid high contrast
- They don’t draw focus

They’re there to create mood if you glance at the screen, not to keep anyone
awake.

## A Simple Sleep Timer

<img
  src='/images/posts/dreamy-sleep/dreamy-sleep-timer.png'
  alt='Dreamy Sleep Timer'
  className='rounded-xl'
/>

One feature I personally rely on is the **sleep timer**.

Instead of letting sound run all night, Dreamy Sleep allows you to choose a
duration:

- 5 minutes
- 10 minutes
- 15 minutes
- 20 minutes
- 30 minutes (default)
- 60 minutes

When the timer ends, playback stops cleanly. This is especially helpful when
sound is only needed during the early phase of sleep—whether for yourself or a
newborn.

---

## Why Not Just Use YouTube?

YouTube works, but it isn’t designed for quiet, predictable night‑time use.

Dreamy Sleep avoids many common frustrations:

- No ads
- No autoplay chains
- No sudden volume changes
- No content recommendations
- No login or subscription

It plays sound, loops it smoothly, and stops when you ask it to.

---

## Built Small, Built Simply

Dreamy Sleep is intentionally lightweight and transparent:

- HTML5 audio
- CSS animations and glassmorphism
- Vanilla JavaScript (ES modules)
- Web Audio API for smooth volume control
- No frameworks or build tools

Everything runs directly in the browser.

---

## Who It’s For

Dreamy Sleep was built for personal use, but it works well for:

- Anyone who wants calm background sound at night
- People sensitive to interruptions or ads
- New parents settling newborns to sleep
- Night‑time routines where simplicity matters
- Light sleepers who prefer predictable audio

---

## Closing Thoughts

Dreamy Sleep isn’t meant to replace large wellness platforms. It’s a small,
focused project—built from a real need for calm, continuous background sound
without distractions.

If it helps others, especially new parents navigating sleepless nights, that’s a
bonus.
